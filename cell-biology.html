Introduction

Cell biology is the study of cells, their physiological properties, structures, organelles, interactions with their environment, life cycle, division, and death.

Main Topics

  • Cell Structure: Plasma membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus
  • Organelles: Mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, lysosomes
  • Cell Division: Mitosis and meiosis
  • Cell Communication: Signals and receptors
  • Cell Transport: Diffusion, osmosis, active transport

Why is Cell Biology Important?

Understanding cells helps scientists and doctors to understand diseases, develop treatments, and explore how life functions at the microscopic level.
